Ayurveda Overview

Ayurveda in Brief

The science of life: balancing Vata, Pitta, and Kapha for well-being.

Ayurveda, the “science of life,” describes health as a dynamic balance of three organizing principles—Vata, Pitta, and Kapha—called doshas. Each person has a unique combination at baseline (their Prakriti). Day-to-day living can shift how the doshas express (your Vikriti).

The Three Doshas

Vata (air/space)

Light, dry, cool, mobile. Imbalance may show dryness, restlessness, variable appetite.

Pitta (fire/water)

Hot, sharp, intense. Imbalance can appear as irritability, overheating, strong appetite, loose stools.

Kapha (earth/water)

Heavy, slow, stable, cool. Imbalance may present as sluggishness, congestion, weight gain.

Five Elements & Core Qualities

Doshas are expressions of five elements (pancha mahabhuta):

Space Air Fire Water Earth

Dosha Elements Key Qualities
Vata Air + Space Light, dry, cool, mobile
Pitta Fire + Water Hot, sharp, oily
Kapha Earth + Water Heavy, slow, stable, cool

We use this as a teaching model, not for diagnosis.

Prakriti vs. Vikriti

Prakriti is your “set point.” Vikriti is your current state. If they differ, support the dosha that’s currently elevated (Vikriti) to move back toward your Prakriti.

Daily Rhythm (Dinacharya) — Snapshot

  • Morning: Wake near sunrise, warm water, brief movement or walk.
  • Midday: Heaviest meal when digestion is strongest.
  • Evening: Lighter dinner, screen wind-down, consistent sleep time.
